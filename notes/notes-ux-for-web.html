User Experience Fundamentals for Web Design

Persona

Find out who will use your site, there attributes, values, goals and concerns.

While creating the side, ask your self. Will this site cover there needs?

What make people leave?

Anything that stops visitors from finding the information they are looking for. So it's importance to make the page easy to scan.

Too much distractions, like ads, etc.

Menu / Navigator

Don't have more then 7 item in an menu, if one have more. Use a separator between them.

Fitts' Law

It's faster to hit larger targets closer to you then smaller targets farther from you.

Elements Every Page Should Have

Every page most show what the site is about. Write a tag line that describes what the page is about. It should contain product and the area your in. The tag should be write with text so the search engine can find it.

  • The first paragraph get the most attention (left-top corner).
  • Put a summary of the page's content in the first paragraph.
  • Make suer the page headings convey useful information.
  • Front load headings and bullet points with the information-carrying words.

People scan the page for useful information to help them before starting to read the hole page.

Keep the text short and simple, easy to read and let it be high on facts.

Write things in an up-side-down pyramid, with the order:

1. Who, what, when, where why and how conclusion.
2. Important details.
3. General information.

To make the text shorter, cut point 2 and 3.

User should be able to say generally what your site is about after 5s. This can be tested with the site: fivescondstest and clueapp.

The About Us page is used to make your self trust worthy, so be honest and show picture about your company and a contact form.

Use diffrent sizes for form inputs depended on how much information is expected.
