Copywriting

It will take time to see results, especially with blogging. See it as an snowball being pushed down a hill, it will be small in the beginning but soon become bigger and bigger. But do get it in the right direction, you have to keep steering it.

Evergreen, keep you post updated over time. Example, if you have written about a software, make sure to update the article when the software is updated.

Who is you nish audience? Try to identify them and try to write for them. Identifying the audience and try to "draw" a picture of that person: What does he/she wants to learn / know?

  1. How old is he/she.
  2. Does he/she have a family?
  3. What are his or hers hobbies?
  4. What does he or she work?
  5. What problem can you solve for him or her?
  6. Where does he hangout on-line?

What is it exactly that you want he/she to do at the end of your blog post?

A good writer who is educating others will translate an area of expertise into a layman's language.

Try to find stupid questions and answer them in your article/blog. Look in forums etc. for these questions.

Make the blog/article easily to scan through.

By sharing the page on Google+ it will be index to google directly, most important social media for SEO.

Avoid the stock look (photos)! "I hate happy people!".

AIDA flow copywriting

  • (A)ttention: Headline (about 3s to get their attention)
  • (I)nterest: Opening paragraph (about 7s to read)
  • (D)esire: Main body text
  • (A)ction: Last paragraph
  • (P)roof: Review and statistic

Answer your self these two questions before starting to write:

  1. What is your client trying to buy?
    1. How do they search? Layman's Terms or Technical terms
    2. What keywords do they use, research keywords.
    3. Answer/quote the anticipated search query.
    4. First part of title.
  2. What will they gain?
    1. Emotional, Intellectual or Physical gain.
    2. Describe that gain as a benefit.

Combined these to answers to a title.

Interest is always developed in the opening paragraph. You have about 7s to drive the readers to the desire content. Keep it to 2 or 3 sentences and ask question(s)!

Desire, the main body part. It's educational in nature and should describe how they will benefit.

Action, should be in the last paragraph. The higher the transaction value, the stronger the action statement. Be very clear about the benefits of the CAT(Call To Action).

Proof, backup your claim and promises with statistic and picture or social proof.
